ORBit is a physics sandbox where you launch planets into orbit around a sun. Only blue and green stable orbits score. The sun grows every time it absorbs a planet — fill its mass bar and it collapses into a black hole, ending the game. Everything follows one rule: mass attracts mass.

Controls

🚀

Launcher

Pull back in any direction and release. Each orb takes longer to load than the last. Once ready, you have 10 seconds to launch — or it auto-fires toward the sun.

🌀

Gravity well

Touch anywhere to create a gravity well. Nearby orbs are pulled toward your finger. Hold still to trap them, move slowly to guide them, flick fast to sling them away.

☀️

The sun

Pulls everything toward it. Orbs that get too close are absorbed — and the penalty escalates every time. Don't feed it.

⚫

Black hole

Every planet the sun absorbs grows its mass. Watch the ☀ mass bar in the score panel. When it fills, the sun collapses — and devours everything on the map. That's game over.

◉

Stability colors

Blue and green = stable, scoring orbits. Yellow → orange → red = decaying, non-scoring. Nudge red orbs back to stability.

Scoring

+n
stable (blue/green) planets only
×n
more orbs = bar fills faster
3+
minimum orbs to start scoring
−5×n
each eaten orb costs more

The sun penalty escalates: 1st orb eaten = −5, 2nd = −10, 3rd = −15. Watch the ☀ tracker in the corner.

Tips

Watch the trajectory line before releasing — it curves toward the sun's gravity.
Launch sideways to create elliptical orbits that cross the screen.
Moving the sun reshapes every orbit at once.
A gentle perpendicular nudge with the gravity well can rescue a red orb without sending it flying.
Launcher cooldown grows with each orb launched — later orbs take up to 60s to load.
Smaller planets add less mass to the sun than large ones — use them to play it safe.
When the mass bar glows purple, the black hole is close. End your game and save your score.
