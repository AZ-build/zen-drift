ORBit

Launch planets into orbit around the sun. Blue orbits score points. The sun grows each time it absorbs a planet — fill its mass bar and it collapses into a black hole. Everything follows one rule: mass attracts mass.

Controls

🚀

Launcher

Pull back in any direction and release. The trajectory line shows where it will go. Use the handle on the right edge to slide the launcher closer or further from the sun — closer means faster orbits. Each planet takes longer to load. You have 10 seconds to launch before it auto-fires toward the sun.

🔍

Zoom

Pinch with two fingers to zoom in or out. Zoom out to see planets that have drifted off-screen. Double-tap to reset zoom.

🌀

Gravity well

Touch and hold anywhere to warp spacetime. The longer you hold, the deeper the well and the stronger the pull. Trail your finger to carve a channel that planets will follow. A light swipe nudges gently — a long hold can catch a fast-moving planet.

☀️

The sun

Pulls everything toward it. Planets that get too close are absorbed — each one costs −10 points and grows the sun's mass. Don't feed it.

⚫

Black hole

Watch the ☀ mass bar in the score panel. When it fills, the sun collapses into a black hole and devours every planet on the map — each one still costs −10. That's game over.

◉

Orbit lines

Each planet's trail and leader line shows its orbit state. Blue = stable, scoring orbit. Yellow = drifting. Red + flashing = falling into the sun — act fast. Only blue orbits score.

Tips

Watch the trajectory line before releasing — a medium pull aimed sideways usually lands a stable orbit.
Slide the launcher handle up to launch closer to the sun — tighter, faster orbits.
Hold your finger in front of an incoming red planet — a deep well can redirect it to safety.
Smaller planets add less mass to the sun. Play small to delay the black hole.
When the mass bar glows red, the black hole is close — end your game and save your score before it's too late.
In Chill mode there's no cooldown, no scoring, and no black hole. Pure physics.
