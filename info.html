ORBit

Gravity Sandbox

A physics playground where you launch planets, build a solar system, and keep everything in orbit. The more stable your system, the higher your score.

Launch ORBit

About

I built ORBit as a gravity sandbox for my kids — a way to play with orbital mechanics without needing to understand the math behind it. Planets launched at the right speed settle into orbits. Launched too fast and they fly away. Too slow and the sun swallows them. Everything emerges from the same simple rule: mass attracts mass. It turns out that's enough to make something endlessly fascinating to mess around with.

How to play

🚀

Launch planets

Pull back on the launcher at the bottom of the screen to charge. Angle left or right across a 180° arc. Release to fire. The further you pull, the faster the launch. Tap the planet in the launcher to cycle through different planet types and sizes.

⬤

Move the puck

Touch anywhere on the screen (outside the launcher) to place a puck. Drag it to push planets around, nudging their orbits. Speed matters — a slow drag barely affects a planet, a fast swipe sends it flying. You can push the sun too.

☀️

Avoid the sun

The sun pulls everything toward it. Planets that get too close are absorbed — that's −10 points. Planets in stable orbits are safe. The spacetime grid warps around the sun, showing its gravitational pull.

◉

Stability colors

Each planet glows based on how stable its orbit is. Blue means a healthy, near-circular orbit. As it destabilizes the color shifts through green → yellow → orange → red. Nudge red planets back toward the sun to calm them down.

You need at least 3 planets in orbit for scoring to begin. More planets means the score ticks up faster.

Tips

Launch sideways to create elliptical orbits that cross different paths — harder to maintain but higher planet count.
Watch the trajectory line before releasing — it shows the actual path your planet will take, curving toward the sun's gravity.
Moving the sun reshapes every orbit at once. A carefully moved sun can rescue a planet heading into it.
Small planets are lighter and easier to deflect. Big ones carry momentum and are harder to redirect once moving.
A planet that turns red doesn't need to die — a gentle perpendicular nudge with the puck can pull it back into a stable orbit.

Add to home screen

ORBit works as a full-screen app with no browser chrome. Install it on your home screen for the best experience.

iOS · Safari

1
Open ORBit.html in Safari on your iPhone or iPad
2
Tap the Share button — the square with an arrow pointing up, at the bottom of the screen
3
Scroll down and tap Add to Home Screen
4
Tap Add — ORBit will appear on your home screen and open full-screen like a native app

Android · Chrome

1
Open ORBit.html in Chrome on your Android device
2
Tap the three-dot menu in the top right corner
3
Tap Add to Home screen
4
Tap Add — ORBit installs as a shortcut with its own icon
